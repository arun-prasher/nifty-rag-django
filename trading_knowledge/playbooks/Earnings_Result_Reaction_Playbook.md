# Earnings Result Reaction Playbook
(NIFTY 50 & Heavyweight Stocks)

## Purpose
To correctly interpret how the market is reacting to earnings results,
using **price, VWAP, and volume**, instead of headlines or opinions.

> Earnings are information.  
> Price action is judgment.

This playbook applies primarily to:
- NIFTY 50 heavyweights
- Index-moving stocks
- Earnings days and post-earnings sessions

---

## Core Principle
Markets trade on **expectations vs reality**, not on absolute profit or revenue numbers.

A ‚Äúgood result‚Äù can still lead to selling  
if expectations were higher.

## The First 15-Minute Rule (Non-Negotiable)

No trades are taken **in the first 15 minutes** after market open on result days.

The first 15 minutes are used only to:
- Observe
- Classify
- Decide bias

---

## Phase-Based Reading Framework

### Phase 1: Minute 0‚Äì5 (The Open)

Observe:
- Gap type:
  - Gap Up
  - Gap Down
  - Flat
- Volume on first 5-min candle:
  - High
  - Average
  - Low
- Price relative to VWAP:
  - Above VWAP
  - Below VWAP

Interpretation:
- Gap + High Volume = Institutional intent
- Gap + Low Volume = Fragile / suspect move

---

### Phase 2: Minute 5‚Äì10 (The Test)

Observe:
- Does price hold the opening high/low?
- Is there a sharp retracement?
- How does price behave near VWAP?

Key signals:
- VWAP rejection = continuation likely
- Clean VWAP reclaim = sentiment shift
- Fast retrace = gap may fill

---

### Phase 3: Minute 10‚Äì15 (The Verdict)

Ask ONE question:
> Where is price relative to VWAP, with volume?

This determines the trade bias for the session.

## Opening Range Rule (15-Minute Structure)

- Mark:
  - High of first 15 minutes
  - Low of first 15 minutes

After 15 minutes:
- Above range + above VWAP ‚Üí Bullish continuation
- Below range + below VWAP ‚Üí Bearish continuation
- Inside range ‚Üí No trade (chop)

---

## Common Earnings-Day Traps

- Trading based on headlines
- Entering in first candle
- Averaging losing trades
- Fighting VWAP
- Expecting trend on low volume

---

## Correct Trading Behaviour on Result Days

- Let the market reveal its bias
- Trade reaction, not opinion
- Reduce position size if volatility spikes
- Book partial profits early
- Respect invalidation signals quickly

---

## Invalidations (Exit Immediately)

- Strong VWAP reclaim against bias
- Opening range break fails
- Broad-based sector reversal
- Heavyweight buying/selling contradicts setup

---

## What NOT to Do

- Do not predict earnings outcome
- Do not argue with price
- Do not assume gap fill
- Do not force trades on mixed signals
